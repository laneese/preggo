Keep Forever

"This journal lives in your browser. Here's how to make sure it stays yours."

Dear friend,

This journal doesn't live in a cloud. It doesn't live on our servers. It doesn't belong to an app that might disappear in three years when the company changes direction or gets acquired or just quietly stops caring.

It lives in your browser. On your device. Under your control.

Everything you've written — every week, every feeling, every photo — is stored locally on this computer or phone. The moment you close the tab, it's still there. The moment you come back, it's all waiting for you exactly as you left it.

But browsers can be cleared. Devices can be replaced. So here's what we want you to do:

Click the button below. It will create a single file — a backup of everything in this journal. Save it somewhere that matters to you. Your Google Drive. Your Dropbox. Emailed to yourself. On a USB drive in a box with her first outfit and the hospital bracelet.

That file is yours. Forever. Open it on any device with a browser — a phone, a tablet, a laptop, a computer you haven't bought yet — and everything will be right there.

We built it this way on purpose. Because this journal deserves to outlast the technology it runs on.

Her daughter might read it at 25.
That's the whole point.

— EverAfterMemoryCo
